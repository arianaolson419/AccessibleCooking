About BlindOven

We recognize that people who are visually impaired often find conventional cooking resources inaccessible. As such, BlindOven compiles accessible recipes written by amateur and professional chefs with visual impairments. We are the most comprehensive source of recipes and cooking tips for the blind, whether you are new to cooking with visual impairments or an experienced chef.

How to use BlindOven

We have two categories of user submitted content -- recipes and tips. You can search for either from the search page, linked in the menu. Tips can range from knowing when your steak is done to measuring out a cup of oil, and related tips will usually be located in recipe pages.

If you want to save recipes or submit recipes, you will have to create an account from our sign-in page.

Why BlindOven?

Cooking resources are dispersed over the internet and in physical cookbooks. It can be difficult to obtain cooking resources geared for people who have vision impairments; most recipes and other instructions are written for people who can rely on visual clues. Most cooking implements are also made to rely on visual indication. Additionally, many recipe websites are difficult or impossible to navigate with a screen reader. Visually impaired cooks need to hunt down applicable recipes, tips, videos, and other resources, and then compile their own knowledge to compensate for the lack of truly accessible recipes and resources. Many cooks also have to improvise ways to make use of common sight-based cues. These resources will be screen reader friendly and may come with audio or visual supplements geared towards audiences who are visually impaired. Users can upload their own recipes, tips, or techniques, and comment on others'. Our goal is to concentrate resources in one place and provide a living community that will allow people who are visually impaired to help one another in the kitchen.
